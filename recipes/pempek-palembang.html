Pempek Palembang


Image by jcomp on Freepik

Description:

Pempek Palembang is a traditional Indonesian savory fish cake delicacy originating from Palembang, South Sumatra. It is made primarily from ground fish (commonly tenggiri or mackerel) mixed with tapioca flour, giving it a chewy and bouncy texture. What makes Pempek unique is its variety of shapes and fillings, the most famous being Pempek Kapal Selam (submarine Pempek), which is stuffed with a whole egg. Other types include Pempek Lenjer (long cylindrical shape), Pempek Adaan (fried round balls), and Pempek Kulit (made with fish skin). Pempek is served with a dark, rich, sweet-sour and spicy sauce called Cuko, made from palm sugar, tamarind, garlic, and chili. It’s typically enjoyed as a snack or light meal and represents one of the most iconic culinary treasures of Indonesia.

Ingredients:

For the Pempek

  • 500 grams of mackerel fish fillet (or any white fish), minced very finely
  • 200 ml cold water
  • 1 tablespoon salt
  • 1 tablespoon sugar
  • 300–350 grams tapioca flour (cassava starch)
  • 2 cloves of garlic, minced (optional)

For the Egg Filling (for Pempek Kapal Selam version):

  • 2–3 boiled eggs, cut into halves or quarters

For the Cuko (Sweet and Sour Spicy Vinegar Sauce):

  • 200 grams palm sugar (or brown sugar), chopped
  • 5 cloves garlic
  • 10–15 bird’s eye chilies (adjust to taste)
  • 2 tablespoons tamarind paste
  • 500 ml water
  • 1 teaspoon salt

Instructions:

Making Pempek:

  1. In a bowl, mix the minced fish with salt, sugar, and water. Stir until smooth and sticky.
  2. Gradually add tapioca flour while kneading lightly until it becomes a smooth dough. Do not over-knead.
  3. For Pempek Lenjer: take a portion of dough and shape it into long cylinders.
  4. For Pempek Kapal Selam: take a ball of dough, flatten it, fill with a piece of boiled egg, then seal and shape like a submarine.
  5. Boil water in a large pot. Add a bit of oil to prevent sticking.
  6. Boil the pempek until they float (about 10–15 minutes), then remove and let them cool.
  7. You can deep-fry the boiled pempek before serving for a crispy texture (optional but traditional).

Making Cuko Sauce:

  1. Boil water, then add chopped palm sugar and tamarind paste.
  2. Blend garlic and chilies, then add to the boiling sauce.
  3. Simmer for 10–15 minutes until fragrant. Strain the sauce for a smooth finish.

Serving:

  • Cut the pempek into bite-sized pieces.
  • Pour the cuko sauce over it or serve as dipping sauce.
  • Can be served with diced cucumber and yellow noodles (optional).
