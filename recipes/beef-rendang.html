Rendang

Description:

Rendang is a rich and tender Indonesian slow-cooked dry curry originating from the Minangkabau ethnic group of West Sumatra. This iconic dish is made by simmering beef with coconut milk and a fragrant blend of spices including lemongrass, galangal, garlic, turmeric, ginger, and chili, until the liquid reduces and the meat becomes deeply caramelized and infused with intense flavor. Traditionally served during ceremonial occasions and festive gatherings, rendang is known not only for its complex taste—spicy, savory, and slightly sweet—but also for its long shelf life due to the cooking technique. Recognized globally, rendang was once voted the “World’s Most Delicious Food” by CNN International’s readers. Its texture is dry and rich, with the beef incredibly soft from hours of slow cooking. Rendang is often enjoyed with steamed rice and complements other Indonesian dishes beautifully.

Ingrdients:

Main:

  • 1 kg (2.2 lbs) beef (chuck or shank), cut into large cubes
  • 400 ml (1 can) coconut milk (preferably thick, unsweetened)
  • 2 cups water
  • 2 stalks lemongrass, smashed
  • 4 kaffir lime leaves, torn
  • 2 turmeric leaves (optional), tied into a knot
  • 2 tbsp tamarind juice or 1 tbsp tamarind paste
  • 1 tbsp palm sugar (or brown sugar)
  • Salt to taste
  • 3 tbsp cooking oil

Spice for The Paste:

  • 8 shallots
  • 5 cloves garlic
  • 5 red chilies (adjust to taste)
  • 3 candlenuts (or substitute with macadamia nuts)
  • 2 cm fresh ginger
  • 2 cm fresh galangal
  • 2 cm fresh turmeric (or 1 tsp turmeric powder)

Instructions:

  1. Prepare the Spice Paste

    2. Blend all spice paste ingredients into a smooth paste. You can add a little oil or water if needed.

  2. Sauté the Paste

    3. Heat oil in a large wok or pot over medium heat. Sauté the spice paste until fragrant, about 5-7 minutes.

  3. Add Aromatics

    4. Add lemongrass, kaffir lime leaves, and turmeric leaves (if using). Stir for 1–2 minutes.

  4. Add Beef

    5. Add beef cubes and cook until all surfaces are browned and well coated with the spices.

  5. Pour in Liquids

    6. Add coconut milk and water. Stir gently. Add tamarind juice, sugar, and salt. Bring to a boil.

  6. Simmer Slowly

    7. Reduce heat to low. Simmer uncovered for 3–4 hours, stirring occasionally to prevent burning. Cook until the liquid has evaporated and the meat is tender and dark brown.

  7. Final Caramelization

    8. As the dish dries out, oil will separate. Stir often so the beef caramelizes in the coconut oil and darkens in color.
